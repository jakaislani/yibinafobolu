About

The Irish Descendants are a Canadian folk group from Newfoundland and Labrador, known for their traditional Celtic sound infused with contemporary elements. The band was formed in the early 1990s and has released several albums that showcase their unique style of music which includes a mix of fiddles, banjos, accordions, guitars, and bodhráns. Some of their most popular songs include “Rattlin’ Bog,” “Barrett’s Privateers,” and “Rocky Road to Dublin.” The Irish Descendants have toured extensively throughout North America, Europe, and Australia and continue to be a beloved group in the Celtic music scene.
